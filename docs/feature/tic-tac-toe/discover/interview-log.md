For each interview, a real log entry would include:

### Participant metadata

- Pseudonym or participant ID (not real name)
- Segment (e.g., "parent of 5yo", "office worker, daily commuter")

### Interview content

- Opening context ("tell me about your day / week")
- Past-behavior questions and verbatim responses

- Commitment signals: introductions offered, follow-ups agreed, willingness-to-pay signals

### Post-interview analysis

- Key quotes flagged for synthesis
- Assumptions confirmed / disconfirmed / newly surfaced
- Contribution to Opportunity tree (which opportunity, importance/satisfaction ratings)
- Contribution to JTBD map
- Red flags (leading questions accidentally asked, compliments accepted as evidence, etc.)

## What Would Have Been Asked (illustrative question plan)

> `[ILLUSTRATIVE]`

### Problem Discovery (Phase 1)

- "Tell me about the last time you played a quick game on your phone or in a browser. What was the situation?"
- "Walk me through what happened from the moment you decided to play to the moment you stopped."
- "What was annoying about it? What did you like?"
- "Have you ever been mid-game and just closed the tab? What made you do that?"
- "Can you think of a time you wanted to play something with a kid or friend right next to you? What did you actually do?"

### JTBD Probing (Phase 1-2)

- "When you have 60 seconds to kill, what are you really trying to accomplish?"
- "What else are you doing in that moment — checking email, waiting for a bus, avoiding a task?"

### Opportunity Importance / Satisfaction (Phase 2)

- "On a scale of 1-10, how important is it to you that [time-to-first-move be near-instant / there be no ads / you can play with the person next to you]?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how satisfied are you with how you currently get that?"

### Solution Value & Usability (Phase 3)

- Show prototype: "Show me how you'd start a game. Think out loud."
- "What did you expect to happen when you clicked X?"
- "Where did you think the difficulty setting would be?"
- Post-task: "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to come back tomorrow?"

### Viability Signals (Phase 4)

- "If this site asked for $1 to remove a (hypothetical) ad, would you pay?" (framed as past behavior: "Have you ever paid to remove ads from a free game?")
- "Would you bookmark this? Would you send it to someone?"

## Red-Flag Answers That Would Have Disqualified Evidence

- "Yeah, I'd totally use that." (future-intent compliment, not evidence)
- "That's a cool idea." (praise, not signal)
- "Everyone I know would want this." (generalization without specifics)
- Any answer to a leading question like "Wouldn't you agree that..."

Scout would have redirected with: "When did you last do something like this?" to get back to past-behavior ground.
